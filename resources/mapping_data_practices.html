Opening Government Data through Mediation:
Exploring Roles, Practices and Strategies of (Potential) Data Intermediary Organisations in India

A study by Sumandro Chattapadhyay
Part of the Exploring the Emerging Impacts of Open Data in Developing Countries research network
Supported by World Wide Web Foundation and International Development Research Centre, Canada

Mapping Data Practices of (Potential) Data Intermediary Organisations in India

Introduction

The study consisted of semi-structured interviews with various non-governmental and non-commercial organisations in India, across sectors, who work with national-scale datasets created by government agencies. This table -- containing details about how these organisations access, use, and share data (understood as raw/disaggregated data) -- gives an overview of the existing data practices of potential data intermediaries in India.

The detailed table of data practices can be accessed here. The file is shared under Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International license as the rest of the research outputs.

To suggest modification of information shared here, or to contribute further information, please write to Sumandro.

Overview of Data Practices

Organisation Domains How is Data Accessed? How is Data Used? How is Data Shared?
Accountability Initiative, Centre for Policy Research Budget and expenditure analysis; making state procedures and decision making structures more visible to public; accountability activism; elementary education. Government agency websites; data shared by government officials (in digital and paper format); collection of data from government offices in person; RTI requests, data products sold by government agencies and other agencies. Budget, expenditure and policy briefs, reports, newspaper articles, infographics (online and printed), collaboration with print media organisations. Collected and compiled datasets are shared through website.
Akvo Foundation Building softwares for resource and project monitoring with a focus on water issues; supporting field-level implementation of same softwares. It does not work with government data. Various organisations use Akvo tools to collect data for their own work (mostly regarding water resources). Data collected using Akvo softwares are displayed on a map-based portal built and managed by Akvo. Use of the actual data is decided upon by the collecting organisation. Data is separately shared by organisations that have collected data.
ASER Centre Monitoring and assessing outcomes of social sector programmes â€“ primary and secondary education, health and nutrition, livelihoods. Data-products bought/downloaded from government agencies (Census of India, National Sample Survey Organisation, National Family and Health Survey, and District Information System for Education). Print publications (Annual Survey of Education Report), Summary Reports (national, state, and district levels), Right to Education Report Cards (state and district levels), online sharing of data. Partial and aggregated (state level) data sets are shared online. Disaggregated data is shared against direct requests for data re-usage (mostly for academic purposes).
Association for Democrative Reforms Sanitising and analysing affidavits (containing personal, financial, criminal data) submitted by electoral candidates (at national, state and city elections); organising and analysing electoral data (votes obtained by candidates, parties, etc.); parliamentary and developmental activities of elected representatives (national level). Website of Election Commission of India, and paliamentary websites (Rajya Sabha and Lok Sabha). Crowdsource incidents of financial expenditure during election campaigns, but not much data. Charts and reports created using the data; create custom data analysis/reports for local/regional organisations (but does not share raw data with them). Analysis, charts, reports and aggregated tables available online and offline, but not in open formats or licenses. Does not share disaggregated data.
Centre for Budget and Governance Accountability Social sector budgeting, with special focus on allocations for women, children, dalits, minorities, advivasis, and on sectors such as eduation, health, water and sanitation, rural development, agriculture and food security etc.; central- and state-level tax structures; center-state fiscal relations; public finance policy. Downloaded from government agency websites, collected from printed annual reports of government agencies, through RTI requests. Budget analysis, policy reports, (one- and two-pager) thematic briefs, online and offline visualisations, cartoons (depicting policy issues). Data is not shared in disaggregated form, but only in the form of reports and analysis.
India Biodiversity Portal, Strand Life Sciences Biodiversity data, with a specific focus on species and location data. Government does not share data on biodiversity. The Portal hosts (in a structured manner) biodiversity data contributed by various organisations, including academic and commercial researchers, and NGOs working on environment-related topics. Contributed data (on details about the observed species, and the location of the species) is made available on the Portal - for browsing through a map-based interface, and for downloading in entirety. All data contributed to the Portal can be downloaded directly. The data is shared under Creative Commons licenses.
India Energy Portal, The Energy and Resources Institute Energy, natural resources, environment (including climate change). Downloaded from government agency websites, bought from government (or from assigned vendors) in data form, bought from data re-sellers, bought from government in printed format (reports, statistical abstracts, etc.), through the TERI members who are part of government committees, etc. Data gathered by TERI from various sources are used by its researchers as part of their works. This data is mostly shared internally. Disaggregated data is not shared by TERI in most cases. It publishes an annual collection of statistical abstracts and analyses titled TERI Energy Data Directory (TEDD). This publication includes the relevant data in open format (spreadsheet). Further, TERI directly shares collected and compiled (disaggregated) data for its clients / project funders.
India Environment Portal, Centre for Science and Environment Environment, water, pollution, climate change, mobility and development. Downloaded from government agency websites, reports published by agencies, bought from government and non-government (who re-sell government data) sources, obtained through RTI requests, through some CSE researchers who are members of government committees. Data is used for internal research, published through online and print publications (such as, Down to Earth) as data tables and infographics. Data is shared through the IEP website, but without significantly sanitising, re-formatting, or re-organising the data.
India Urban Portal, National Institute of Urban Affairs Urban development and infrastructures, housing, urban sanitation and public health, urban budgets and financing, funding given by central government for city-level projects. Data, mostly related to various city-level projects funded (or co-funded) by central government, and related reports are shared directly by government agencies (both city- and central-level) to be hosted in the Portal. Data is also collected by NIUA researcher in the context of individual studies undertaken by them, but such data is not hosted in the Portal. Data is used for internal research, for monitoring and analysing city-level projects funded by central government, and is hosted on the Portal. Shares data submitted by city-level and central government agencies through the Portal. Most of this data is regarding details and status of city-level projects supported by central government. Data is also directly shared with academic researchers, decided upon in a case-to-case basis.
India Water Portal, Arghyam Water and sanitation. Downloaded from government agency websites, scraped and copied from agency websites, and bought from government agencies. Also contributed to the Portal directly by non-government organisations, academic researchers, and individual collectors of data (say, farmers). Collecting existing data (over Internet) and making them sanitised, machine-readable, and well-documented is the key task of the data team. The cleaned up data is also significantly used internally for research and optimising interventions. The cleaned up data is shared in open formats through the Portal, which functions as a catalog of available data (on the Portal and in other websites) in water and sanitation sector.
Hyderabad Urban Lab Urban political economy, culture and history. Enabling citizens to raise critical urban questions. Information missing. Information missing. Information missing.
Karnataka Learning Partnership Primary and secondary education. Good working relationship with government agencies, especially at the state level, allows KLP to directly access government data from the agencies. Downloads from government agency websites. Website has a map-based platform for visualising school-level education data. Report cards (across scales, ranging from individual schools to the entire state) are developed, printed and shared with various stakeholders. Information gathered from the data is also shared through mobile phone based queries. Disaggregated (school-level) data can be downloaded from the website in open format.
PRS Legislative Research Making the legislative process more informed, transparent and participatory; support legislative and policy making with research (shared with policy makers); making legislative and parliamentary data and information more accessible. Downloaded from websites of government agencies, scraped from websites of the same. Use the data for analysis of legislative and parliamentary activities; public re-sharing of data for easier access to such information. Re-shares government data through its website.
Transparent Chennai, Institute for Financial Management and Research Public health and sanitation, solid waste, water, mobility, neighbourhood-level planning. Downloaded from government agency websites, response to RTI requests, directly collected from agency offices. Data collected through mapping and social surveys, using handheld devices, involving volunteers, interns, and also from government officials knowledgible about ground situations. Maps and data analysis for creating data-driven conversations between various stakeholders, including government. Non-sensitive data is shared through the website.
